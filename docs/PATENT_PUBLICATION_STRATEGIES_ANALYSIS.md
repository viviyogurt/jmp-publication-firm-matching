## Executive Summary

This document analyzes the heterogeneous strategies firms use when choosing between patenting and publishing research. Using a matched sample of 2,202 firms that engage in **both** patenting and academic publishing, we examine:

1. **Temporal trends** in patent vs. publication strategies over 35 years
2. **Firm-level heterogeneity** in innovation strategy
3. **Activity levels** and ratios of patents to publications

**Key Finding:** Firms have dramatically increased their use of BOTH patenting and publishing over time, with a 30.5 percentage point increase in firms doing both activities (from 2.4% in 1990-1994 to 32.9% in 2020-2024).

## ðŸ“Š Methodology

### Sample Construction
1. Start with all CRSP firms (18,709)
2. Identify firms with BOTH patents and publications (2,202 firms)
3. Create firm-year panels for both datasets
4. Merge on GVKEY and year (outer join)
5. Calculate ratios and strategy metrics

### Strategy Classification
- **Patent-focused:** Patents/paper > median (6.0)
- **Publication-focused:** Papers/patent > median
- **Balanced:** Neither extreme

### Period Analysis
- 5-year periods (1990-1994, 1995-1999, etc.)
- Focus on post-1990 (publication data quality)
- Exclude 2025 (incomplete year)
