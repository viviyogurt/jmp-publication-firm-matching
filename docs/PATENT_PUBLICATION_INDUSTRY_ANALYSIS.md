## Executive Summary

This document analyzes how patent vs. publication strategies vary **by industry**, examining 2,202 firms that engage in **both** patenting and academic publishing across **7 broad industry categories**.

**Key Finding:** There is **substantial industry heterogeneity** in innovation strategies:
- **Finance/Banking:** Most patent-focused (689 patents per paper)
- **Technology/Software:** Most publication-focused relative to patents (0.10 papers per patent)
- **Pharma/Biotech:** Balanced but patent-heavy (104 patents, 5 papers per firm)

## ðŸ“Š Methodology

### Industry Classification

**Keyword-Based Classification** (7 categories):
1. Extract keywords from business descriptions and company names
2. Match to industry-specific keyword lists
3. Assign to mutually exclusive categories
4. Fallback to "Other" if no keywords match

**Keyword Lists:**
- **Technology/Software:** software, semiconductor, AI, cloud, internet
- **Pharma/Biotech:** pharmaceutical, drug, biotech, medical, clinical
- **Finance/Banking:** bank, financial, insurance, investment
- **Energy/Utilities:** oil, gas, energy, utility, power
- **Manufacturing/Industrial:** manufacturing, industrial, machinery
- **Retail/Consumer:** retail, consumer, restaurant, e-commerce
- **Other:** None of the above

### Strategy Classification

Firms classified into 3 strategy types based on patent-publication ratios:
- **Patent-focused:** patents/paper > 10
- **Publication-focused:** papers/patent > 0.5
- **Balanced:** Neither extreme

### Limitations

1. **Keyword classification:** May misclassify some firms
2. **Broad categories:** No sub-industry detail (e.g., biotech vs. pharma)
3. **Small "Other" category:** 15% of firms, likely heterogeneous
4. **No SIC codes:** Would provide more precise industry classification
